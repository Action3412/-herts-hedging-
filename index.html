Popular Species

Four hedges, four characters

Most clients ask about these four. Each has a distinct personality — formal or relaxed, dark or bright, classical or contemporary.

Vibrant

Photinia Red Robin

Photinia × fraseri

Glossy green leaves with striking red young shoots. Fast-growing, dense — ideal for screening with year-round colour.

Formal

Portuguese Laurel

Prunus lusitanica

Compact, dense growth with dark glossy foliage and maroon stems. The discerning choice for formal boundaries.

Classical

English Yew

Taxus baccata

The quintessentially formal hedge. Dense, evergreen, with narrow leathery leaves and bright new growth in spring.

Glossy

Cherry Laurel

Prunus laurocerasus

Large, rich glossy leaves on vigorous growth. A relaxed, abundant evergreen for screening and shelter.

Our Process

From nursery to fully established

Step 01

The right plant, year-round

Most root-ball hedging is supplied between September and March. Our Airpot nursery extends that to all twelve months — so you plant when you're ready, not when the season dictates.

  • Bare-root, root-ball and containerised forms
  • Year-round availability via Airpot
  • Specimens hand-selected for form and density
01 · Supply
Step 02

Installation, done right

Mature hedging is a long-term investment. There are golden rules — soil prep, root orientation, depth, spacing, water management — that quietly decide whether it thrives or struggles. We follow them.

  • Site assessment and soil preparation
  • Correct depth, spacing and orientation
  • Stakes, ties and irrigation guidance
02 · Plant
Step 03

Maintenance, species-specific

Over-management or cutting at the wrong time can be as damaging as neglect. Each species has its own schedule, technique and tolerance — and we work to it, not to a generic calendar.

  • Seasonal trimming and feeding programmes
  • Restoration of overgrown hedges
  • Renovation cuts when reduction is needed
03 · Maintain

Our services

Everything your hedge needs

Premium sourcing, expert installation, species-specific ongoing care.

01

Supply

Hedging comes in three forms: bare-root, root-ball and containerised. Each suits a different combination of site, season, species and budget. Root-ball is supplied from mid-September to the end of March — but our Airpot-grown stock is available year-round, in mature sizes that give instant screening and security.

  • Containerised, root-ball and bare-root forms
  • Year-round availability via Airpot
  • European specialist nursery partnerships
  • Immediate screening at any height

02

Plant

Hedges are planted for screening, garden structure, boundary definition, security, or wildlife habitat. The key to long-term success is in the planting itself. Soil preparation, depth, spacing, orientation, watering — there are golden rules, and adhering to them is the difference between a thriving hedge and a struggling one.

  • Professional site assessment
  • Correct depth, spacing and orientation
  • Stakes, ties and irrigation setup
  • Aftercare guidance for the first year
