Common questions

Frequently asked

Straight answers to the questions clients ask most. Anything we haven't covered? Get in touch. We're happy to advise.

When is the best time to plant a hedge?+

Container-grown specimens can be planted year-round, but autumn (October to early December) and early spring (March to April) are ideal: soil is workable, rainfall does most of the watering for you, and roots establish before the demands of summer growth. Root-balled stock has a tighter window of late autumn to early spring.

How quickly will a new hedge look established?+

That's the advantage of working with mature stock: we typically plant specimens that are already 1.8m to 2.5m tall, so the visual impact is immediate. Full knitting-together of the canopy then happens over the first 12 to 24 months as plants settle and put on new growth.

Which species do you most often recommend?+

It depends on site, aspect, soil and the look you're after. Portuguese Laurel (Prunus lusitanica) is our most-requested for its glossy evergreen leaf and reliable habit. English Yew makes a magnificent formal hedge for traditional gardens. Photinia 'Red Robin' is excellent for a touch of colour. We'll always walk the site with you before recommending.

Do you guarantee the plants you supply?+

Yes. When we both supply and plant, we guarantee establishment subject to our aftercare schedule being followed. We provide written guidance on watering, mulching and seasonal care, and we offer optional maintenance plans for clients who'd rather we handle it.

What areas do you cover?+

Hertfordshire and the surrounding Home Counties: Bedfordshire, Buckinghamshire, north London, west Essex and south Northamptonshire. For larger projects we travel further. The Service Area section above lists the towns we visit most often.

How does pricing work?+

Every project is quoted individually. Cost depends on species, specimen size, length of run, site access and whether you'd like us to supply only or also plant. After a free site visit we'll send a clear written quote with no obligation. There's a separate line for supply, planting and any prep work, so you can adjust the scope to suit your budget.
