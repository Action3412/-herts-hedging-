Concept 06 · the logo

The Wordmark

Faithful recreation of the existing logo. The original font is ITC Avant Garde Gothic Bold (1970, Herb Lubalin) — confirmed from the embedded font metadata in your business-card PDF. Avant Garde is a commercial typeface, so I've shown a free Google Font alternative (Outfit 800) as the closest match, plus Lato Black for comparison. The interlock trick: "Hedging" is shifted right by exactly one H-width so the right pillar of "Hertfordshire"-H and the left pillar of "Hedging"-H share a single vertical column. Line-height is set to match the cap-height ratio so the H's meet end-to-end.

Notes
  • Original font: ITC Avant Garde Gothic Bold (1970, Herb Lubalin) — confirmed via the embedded font metadata in your business-card PDF. The same font runs through the whole brand identity (van, card, sign).
  • Avant Garde is commercial. Web licensing costs roughly £50-200 (Adobe Fonts, Linotype, Monotype). Three options: (a) license it for a pixel-perfect match, (b) use the closest free Google Font alternative — Outfit 800 (recommended), or (c) commission a designer to convert the existing logo to SVG paths so we never depend on the font file at all.
  • Outfit 800 is the closest free match — modern geometric grotesque from Indian Type Foundry. Matches Avant Garde's cap-height ratio (0.74), geometric construction, and stroke weight more closely than Lato or Open Sans.
  • The interlocking trick: "Hedging" is offset right by exactly one H-width via the CSS custom property --hh-shift, so the right pillar of H₁ and the left pillar of H₂ share a vertical column. Line-height is set to each font's cap-height ratio (--cap-h) so H₁'s baseline meets H₂'s cap-top exactly — one unbroken stroke through both H's.
  • For the live site, the wordmark would replace the SVG leaf-mark currently in the nav and footer — type-only. Optional: keep the leaf mark as a small accent or as the favicon only.

Notes
  • Variant A — Shared spine: Two H's joined at the centre, gold cross-bars stepped down. Most "monogram"-like.
  • Variant B — Woven bars: Four upright strokes with two cross-bars at different heights — feels like the H's literally pass through each other.
  • Variant C — Stacked: One H above another, mirroring the way "Hertfordshire / Hedging" sits on the van. Strongest brand-system tie.
  • All three are paired with the van's wordmark style — italic Inter, "Hertfordshire" smaller above a bigger "Hedging".
  • For the live site we'd swap Inter italic for a closer match to the actual van font if you can identify it (likely a humanist italic — Avenir Next Italic or similar). Let me know if you want me to source the exact typeface.
